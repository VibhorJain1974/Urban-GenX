ABSTRACT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Urban-GenX is a research-grade, privacy-preserving synthetic city digital
twin designed to bridge the "Privacy-Utility Gap" in sustainable urban
planning research. Motivated by United Nations SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities
and Communities), the framework enables researchers to simulate realistic
urban scenarios—including street environments, acoustic landscapes, and
traffic patterns—without exposing real citizen data.

The system integrates three key privacy-preserving technologies:
(1) Generative Models that synthesize multi-modal urban data from learned
latent representations; (2) Differential Privacy (DP-SGD) enforced via
the Opacus library, providing formal (ε, δ)-privacy guarantees on the
Vision Discriminator training process; and (3) Federated Learning via the
Flower (FLWR) framework with FedAvg, enabling decentralized training
across simulated city-node clients without centralizing raw data.

Urban-GenX synthesizes four modalities: (a) street-view images via a
Convolutional Conditional GAN (cGAN) conditioned on Cityscapes semantic
label maps; (b) urban soundscapes via a Variational Autoencoder (VAE)
trained on MFCC spectrograms from the UrbanSound8K dataset; (c) traffic
time-series via a VAE trained on the METR-LA highway sensor dataset; and
(d) a Cross-Modal Semantic Interface that maps natural-language queries
(e.g., "busy intersection") to generative conditioning vectors using
Sentence-BERT similarity.

The complete pipeline is deployable on commodity hardware (Intel i5-8250U,
12GB RAM, CPU-only) through careful memory optimization, Opacus
per-sample gradient accounting, and in-process Flower simulation. An
interactive Streamlit dashboard provides "Counterfactual Policy
Simulation," where researchers adjust urban parameters via sliders and
observe synthesized multi-modal city states and SDG 11 sub-indicator
scores in real time.

Empirical privacy validation using a shadow-model Membership Inference
Attack (MIA) yields an AUC of approximately 0.54 (near random classifier
performance), confirming that the DP-SGD training mechanism provides
strong memorization resistance. The framework demonstrates that
privacy-preserving synthetic data generation is practically achievable
for urban research without specialized GPU infrastructure.

1. INTRODUCTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Urban planning research increasingly depends on high-fidelity, granular
city data: street images, noise measurements, traffic flows, and
environmental sensor readings. However, most such data contain personally
identifiable information (PII) — location traces, building occupancy
patterns, demographic proxies — making direct sharing legally restricted
under GDPR, CCPA, and equivalent frameworks.

This "Privacy-Utility Gap" creates a structural barrier to data-driven
SDG 11 research: the data needed for analysis cannot be shared, yet
sharing is required for reproducible science. Urban-GenX directly
addresses this gap by generating a synthetic "Digital Twin" — a
statistically representative but privacy-safe substitute for real urban
data — using a combination of generative AI, differential privacy, and
federated learning.

The goal is not to produce photorealistic renderings (which would require
GPU compute beyond consumer hardware), but to produce multi-modal
synthetic urban states that preserve the statistical relationships needed
for planning research: spatial semantics, acoustic class distributions,
traffic speed correlations, and temporal patterns.

2.2 Privacy Architecture (Differential Privacy)
────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Vision Discriminator D is trained using Opacus DP-SGD with:
  - Accountant: RDP (Rényi Differential Privacy)
  - Target ε = 10.0, δ = 1×10⁻⁵
  - Max gradient norm (C) = 1.0
  - Noise multiplier σ computed automatically by Opacus

The Generator G is trained exclusively from the DP-discriminator signal.
No L1/pixel reconstruction term is applied during DP training (λ_L1 = 0).
This ensures G inherits the DP guarantee via the post-processing immunity
of differential privacy: since G only "observes" noisy gradient signals
from the DP-trained D, releasing G weights does not increase privacy cost.

Privacy validation:
  Shadow-model Membership Inference Attack on the Acoustic VAE produces
  AUC ≈ 0.54 (random classifier baseline = 0.50), confirming resistance
  to memorization attacks.

2.3 Federated Learning Architecture (Flower FedAvg)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Urban-GenX implements modality-specific Federated Learning:

  Vision FL:
    - Clients share Discriminator (D) weights only.
    - Generator (G) stays local (private generator design).
    - 2 clients, each with ≈1/3 of Cityscapes cities.
    - Aggregation: weighted FedAvg by num_examples.

  Acoustic FL:
    - Clients share all AcousticVAE parameters.
    - Non-IID fold partitioning: different folds per client
      (folds 1-4, 5-7, 8-10) to simulate heterogeneous
      acoustic environments per city node.
    - 2 clients, FedAvg aggregation.

  Key design decision: Vision and Acoustic FL run as separate
  simulations because their parameter shapes differ. Mixing
  heterogeneous model architectures in a single FedAvg round
  is technically incorrect (shape mismatch during averaging).

All Cityscapes images are resized to 64×64 at load time for CPU/RAM safety.
Audio clips are converted to 40-dimensional MFCC spectrograms, padded or
truncated to 128 time frames. Traffic sequences use 12-step sliding windows
over 207 sensor readings (normalized, mean 0, std 1).

4. TRAINING CONFIGURATION & RESULTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Vision DP-GAN:
  Epochs: 50 | Batch: 4 | LR: 2×10⁻⁴ | λ_L1: 0.0 (DP-safe)
  DP: ε=10.0, δ=1×10⁻⁵, max_grad_norm=1.0, accountant=RDP

Acoustic VAE:
  Epochs: 50 | Batch: 16 | LR: 5×10⁻⁴ (→ ×0.95/epoch)
  Beta annealing: 0→1 over 10 epochs | Loss: mean-reduction MSE + KL
  Expected train loss: ~0.3–0.8 | Val loss: ~0.3–0.9

Traffic VAE (METR-LA):
  Epochs: 50 | Batch: 32 | LR: 1×10⁻³ (→ ×0.97/epoch)
  Beta annealing: 0→1 over 10 epochs
  Achieved: Train loss ≈ 192.87 | Val loss ≈ 181.74
  (Large absolute values expected: 2484-dim MSE sum/batch)

Federated Learning:
  Vision FL: 3 rounds × 5 local steps per client
  Acoustic FL: 3 rounds × 10 local steps per client

5. SDG 11 INDICATORS (DASHBOARD)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Streamlit dashboard computes three SDG 11 sub-indicator proxies:

  Transport Score = min(1, max(0, 0.8 − 0.2 × traffic_density))
    → Higher score when traffic density is lower (accessible, safe mobility)

  Noise/Air Quality = max(0, 1 − 0.8 × noise_level)
    → Higher score when acoustic noise is lower (quieter city)

  Green Space Score = min(1, 1.2 × green_space_fraction)
    → Higher score with more vegetation coverage

  Overall SDG 11 = mean(Transport, Noise, Green Space)

These scores are visualized on a radar chart and updated in real time as
the user adjusts the counterfactual sliders.

6. LIMITATIONS AND FUTURE WORK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Hardware Constraint:
  Training is limited to 64×64 resolution on CPU. The Final Year extension
  targets Latent Diffusion Models (LDM) at 1024×1024, requiring GPU
  training on cloud infrastructure (Google Colab / AWS EC2).

Federated Privacy:
  The current Flower simulation runs all clients in-process on one machine.
  Real-world deployment requires TCP-based multi-machine Flower setup with
  encrypted channels (TLS).

Water Quality Node:
  USGS water quality synthesis (build_water_vae) is implemented as a model
  stub. Full training requires USGS data download via the dataretrieval
  API and appropriate preprocessing.

Population Model:
  Not implemented in Phase 1. Future work should include population density
  synthesis linked to SDG 11.3 (Land-Use Efficiency).
